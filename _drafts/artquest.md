# Learning to Draw for Engineers

I have started this draft at what might as well be the start of my journey.
My whole life I have been a serial doodler, but nothing more. Not to say I
haven't improved but no technique really, just feeling it out. Then I saw
[this fantastic post][weeping-stag-post] and I have tangible advice. Clark
has given me a nice little road map now. So let's lay it out the important
bits.

- Figure drawing at least 15 to 30 minutes a day! It's so obvious in retrospect.
The human body is the most complex collection of shapes to draw that obviously
it's the best way to practice.
- Draw a lot and draw whatever you want. Basically what I was already doing.
I was learning a bit from trying to copy my favourite artist (like the legendary
Carl Barks).
- Be a goldfish. Forget your last drawing and focus on your next. That's just
good advice for getting better at anything
- Long term projects. Having a long term goal to work on with no united style
and its own prompts is a great way to feel like you are doing something and
making good progress. For me this is a chance to kill two birds with one stone
as I have always wanted to get proper into Netrunner. Wouldn't it be great to
make a full deck of proxies (well 2 decks since you need a corpo and a
netrunner). So I am picking 2 decks off [NetrunnerDB][netrunnerdb] that I
intend to play.

I think it's also important to lay out why I would even try; to remind myself.
Since I have become a father one of my favourite things to do is draw with my
son (my daughter is currently still to young for this). Being able to draw one
of his favourite characters and hear his excitement as he recognises who it is;
nothing beats it. I never want that to go away. To my mind the only way to
guarantee that is to make sure I get better at drawing before he learns to recognise
my shody work. I need to just be ahead of his developing taste.

Additionally, everything I love spending my free time on needs art. TTRPGs, comics
and video games all need art. I want to be able to contribute to and give back
to these art forms that mean so much to me. Going back way into my childhood I
have always wanted to make comics, but I just lacked the capacity to draw them.
I want to draw them still, and maybe I can with just a bit of practice. Some
part of me even naively hopes to be able to make a living of such things and
to that end I will never find out if I can if I don't try.

and look back at how far I've come.
