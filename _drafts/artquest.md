# Learning to Draw for Engineers

I have started this draft at what might as well be the start of my journey. My whole life I have been a serial doodler, but nothing more. Not to say I haven't improved but no technique really, just feeling it out. Then I saw [this fantastic post][weeping-stag-post] and I have tangible advice. Clark has given me a nice little road map now. So let's lay it out the important bits.

- Figure drawing at least 15 to 30 minutes a day! It's so obvious in retrospect. The human body is the most complex collection of shapes to draw that obviously it's the best way to practice.
- Draw a lot and draw whatever you want. Basically what I was already doing. I was learning a bit from trying to copy my favourite artist (like the legendary Carl Barks).
- Be a goldfish. Forget your last drawing and focus on your next.
- Long term projects. Having a long term goal to work on with no united style and its own prompts is a great way to feel like you are doing something and making good progress. For me this is a chance to kill two birds with one stone as I have always wanted to get proper into Netrunner. Wouldn't it be great to make a full deck of proxies (well 2 decks since you need a corpo and a netrunner). So I am picking 2 decks off [NetrunnerDB][netrunnerdb] that I intend to play.
