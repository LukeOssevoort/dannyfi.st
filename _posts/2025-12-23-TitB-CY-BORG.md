I finally did it! I converted my silly little Cyberpunk Red screamsheet into a CY_BORG Zungeon - for Zungeon Jam. You can find it on itch below

Is it particularly punk to lay out your zungeon in a markup language? Probably not. But it is in the sense that it is mine, and how I would do it.

Now I am going to get a little personal for a bit. This stupid little zungeon started off as a little piece in my back pocket for a Cyberpunk Red game I was running for my friends. Sort of an early toe dip into the OSR. That game fell apart before I could bring this to the table. I only resurrected it when I started running Red for money on Startplaying. Then I saw its potential.

Long story short I typed it up, realised I couldn't put it on itch due to RTGs content policy and put it out on their Discord and this blog instead. I knew I wanted to give it another crack for a few reasons but couldn't build up the motivation. Between work and kids I was cracking under the pressure and my hobbies suffered.

A few mental health crises later and I finally started seeing a therapist. So far I have only been twice but the changes are already pretty big for me. One of the first strategies we discussed was a flow activity between work and home to reenergise me - something I liked doing that gave me energy, writing dungeons [^tism]. Now I am sitting there on the train doodling away in my little notebook and coming home ready for almost anything. Bonus, I get a bunch of bite-sized dungeons[^bsd] sitting there ready for game night. 

After a couple of weeks of doing this I started turning my mind back to [Terror in the Biodome](https://dannyfi.st/posts/Terror-in-the-Biodome/) and finally converting it for CY_BORG. Before you know it I have a newly drawn map, 3 more rooms, NPC stats, and Cairn 2e style encounter/sign/environment tables. So I pivoted on my half done Zungeon Jam submission that I resented and just choose to finish this.

For me it is enough to know that I finished the text, gave people something gameable, and submitted something to the jam. I don't know, and don't really care if it is good (though I do hope people enjoy it). This is my first proper OSR module! I did it and before the end of a really chaotic and challenging year. That's enough for me for now.
